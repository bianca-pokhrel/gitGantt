Documentation for: gitGantt... a new way to plan your week

When working on short term projects, it is often a challenge to plan tasks and measure progress. gitGantt allows you to incorporate Gantt charts with regular day-to-day to-do lists to provide you with the ultimate way of keeping your projects on schedule. With gitGantt you can add, remove, and edit tasks

Creating an empty gitGantt chart

To create an empty gitGantt chart, please call the constructor with no parameters. An example is provided below:


chart.addTasks(duration, taskName)

The addTasks method in gitGantt allows developers to quickly and efficiently add tasks to their gitGantt chart. These tasks are in the form of list items and are thus easily accessible through the DOM. Please see the example below to see how you can add a task.


chart.deleteTasks(taskID)

The deleteTasks method in gitGantt allows developers to quickly and efficiently delete tasks to their gitGantt chart. These tasks are in the form of list items and are thus easily accessible through the DOM. To delete a task, simply input the taskID. Task IDs are sequential starting from 0. Thus, if you want to delete the 3rd task from your gitGantt chart, you pass 2 as your id. See the example below:


chart.editTasks(taskID)

The editTasks method in gitGantt allows developers to quickly and efficiently edit tasks in their gitGantt chart. To edit a task, simply include the taskID you want to edit, and either the duration you want to edit or the task name you want to edit or both! These tasks are in the form of list items and are thus easily accessible through the DOM. Thus, if you want to edit the 3rd task from your gitGantt chart, you pass 2 as your id and your respective duration and/or name edits. See the example below:


chart.changeTheme(themeName)
